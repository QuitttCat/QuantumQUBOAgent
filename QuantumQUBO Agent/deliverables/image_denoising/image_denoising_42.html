Binary Image Denoising

Problem Statement

Given a noisy binary image represented on a grid of $N$ pixels, the objective is to recover a clean binary image that minimizes a global energy function. This energy comprises two components: a data fidelity term that penalizes deviations between the reconstructed pixels and the observed noisy pixels, and a smoothness term that penalizes label discontinuities between spatially adjacent pixels. The task is to determine the binary configuration that achieves the minimum total energy.

Decision Variables

Let $\mathcal{K} = \{0, 1, \dots, N-1\}$ denote the set of indices corresponding to the pixels in the image grid. For each $k \in \mathcal{K}$, we introduce a binary decision variable $x_k \in \{0,1\}$, where $x_k = 1$ indicates a foreground (active) pixel and $x_k = 0$ indicates a background (inactive) pixel in the reconstructed clean image. The vector of all variables is denoted $\mathbf{x} = (x_0, x_1, \dots, x_{N-1})^\top$.

QUBO Derivation

We derive the Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization (QUBO) formulation through the following algebraic steps. Step 1. The original objective is expressed as a minimization over the binary hypercube: \begin{align} \min_{\mathbf{x} \in \{0,1\}^N} \quad & \sum_{k \in \mathcal{K}} d_k(x_k) + \sum_{(k,l) \in \mathcal{E}} w_{kl} |x_k - x_l|, \end{align} where $\mathcal{E}$ is the set of adjacent pixel pairs, $w_{kl} \geq 0$ are smoothness weights, and $d_k(x_k)$ is the instance-dependent data cost for pixel $k$. Step 2. The problem formulation contains no explicit equality or inequality constraints. Consequently, the constraint set is empty, and no constraint equations are introduced. Step 3. Since there are no constraints, penalty terms are omitted. We proceed to convert the objective terms into a quadratic polynomial using the idempotent property of binary variables, $x_k^2 = x_k$. For binary variables, the absolute difference simplifies to: \begin{align} |x_k - x_l| = x_k + x_l - 2x_k x_l. \end{align} The data cost $d_k(x_k)$ can be represented by its linear coefficient $\delta_k$ such that $d_k(x_k) = \delta_k x_k$ (constant offsets are absorbed into the global offset). Substituting these expansions into the objective yields: \begin{align} H(\mathbf{x}) = \sum_{k \in \mathcal{K}} \delta_k x_k + \sum_{(k,l) \in \mathcal{E}} w_{kl} (x_k + x_l - 2x_k x_l). \end{align} Step 4. We combine and collect terms by variable index. Each quadratic term $x_k x_l$ appears exactly once in the edge sum. Each linear term $x_k$ accumulates contributions from its own data cost and from every incident edge $(k,l) \in \mathcal{E}$: \begin{align} H(\mathbf{x}) = \sum_{k \in \mathcal{K}} \left( \delta_k + \sum_{l \in \mathcal{N}(k)} w_{kl} \right) x_k - \sum_{(k,l) \in \mathcal{E}} 2w_{kl} x_k x_l, \end{align} where $\mathcal{N}(k) = \{ l \mid (k,l) \in \mathcal{E} \}$ denotes the set of neighbors of pixel $k$. Step 5. Reading off the coefficients from the expanded Hamiltonian $H(\mathbf{x}) = \mathbf{x}^\top Q \mathbf{x} + c$, we obtain the general closed-form expressions for the QUBO parameters: \begin{align} Q_{k,k} &= \delta_k + \sum_{l \in \mathcal{N}(k)} w_{kl}, \quad \forall k \in \mathcal{K}, \\ Q_{k,l} &= -2w_{kl}, \quad \forall (k,l) \in \mathcal{E}, \\ Q_{k,l} &= 0, \quad \forall (k,l) \notin \mathcal{E}, \, k \neq l, \\ c &= 0. \end{align}

Penalty Weight Justification

The problem definition specifies no explicit constraints; therefore, penalty terms and associated penalty weights are omitted from the formulation. The optimization is strictly unconstrained over $\{0,1\}^N$. If regularization or hard constraints were introduced in a variant of this problem, the penalty weight $\lambda$ would be chosen to satisfy $\lambda > \max_{\mathbf{x}} |H_{\text{obj}}(\mathbf{x})|$, ensuring that any violation of feasibility incurs an energy penalty strictly larger than the maximum possible reduction in the objective function.

Correctness Argument

Minimizing $H(\mathbf{x})$ over $\{0,1\}^N$ exactly recovers the optimal clean image because the QUBO is an algebraically equivalent reformulation of the original energy function. The expansion using $x_k^2 = x_k$ preserves the exact numerical value of the objective for every binary configuration, meaning the relative ordering of all $2^N$ candidate solutions remains unchanged. Since the problem is unconstrained, every binary vector is feasible, and the global minimum of $H(\mathbf{x})$ corresponds precisely to the configuration that minimizes the combined data fidelity and smoothness costs. The quadratic structure ensures the energy landscape is well-defined for both classical and quantum solvers without introducing artificial feasible regions or penalty-induced local minima.
