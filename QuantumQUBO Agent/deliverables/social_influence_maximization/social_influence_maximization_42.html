Social Influence Maximization

Problem Statement

Given a social network with $N$ candidate seed nodes, each associated with an individual influence reach $w_i$ and a pairwise overlap $w_{ij}$ quantifying the number of users influenced by both seed $i$ and seed $j$, the objective is to select exactly $K$ seed nodes to maximize the total unique influence coverage. The optimization must account for individual reach while penalizing double-counting of users reached by multiple selected seeds.

Decision Variables

Let $x_i \in \{0,1\}$ for each candidate seed node $i \in \{0, 1, \dots, N-1\}$ be a binary decision variable, where $x_i = 1$ indicates that seed node $i$ is selected, and $x_i = 0$ indicates it is not selected.

QUBO Derivation

Step 1. The original problem is a maximization. To cast it as a QUBO minimization, we negate the objective function: \begin{align} \min_{\mathbf{x} \in \{0,1\}^N} \quad -\sum_{i=0}^{N-1} w_i x_i + \lambda \sum_{0 \le i < j \le N-1} w_{ij} x_i x_j, \end{align} where $\lambda > 0$ is the penalty weight for double-counting overlap. Step 2. The cardinality constraint requires exactly $K$ seeds to be selected: \begin{align} \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} x_i = K. \end{align} Step 3. We convert the constraint to a penalty term with weight $P > 0$: \begin{align} \text{Penalty}(\mathbf{x}) &= P \left( \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} x_i - K \right)^2. \end{align} Expanding the square yields: \begin{align} \left( \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} x_i - K \right)^2 &= \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} x_i^2 - 2K \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} x_i + K^2 + \sum_{i \neq j} x_i x_j. \end{align} Using the idempotent property of binary variables, $x_i^2 = x_i$, and noting that $\sum_{i \neq j} x_i x_j = 2 \sum_{0 \le i < j \le N-1} x_i x_j$, we obtain: \begin{align} \left( \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} x_i - K \right)^2 &= \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} (1 - 2K) x_i + 2 \sum_{0 \le i < j \le N-1} x_i x_j + K^2. \end{align} Multiplying by $P$ gives the general penalty contribution to the Hamiltonian: \begin{align} \text{Penalty}(\mathbf{x}) &= P \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} (1 - 2K) x_i + 2P \sum_{0 \le i < j \le N-1} x_i x_j + P K^2. \end{align} Step 4.
