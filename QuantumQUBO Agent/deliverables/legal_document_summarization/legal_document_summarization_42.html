Problem Statement

Given a legal document partitioned into $N$ candidate sentences, the goal is to select exactly $K$ sentences to form an extractive summary. Each sentence $i \in \{0, 1, \dots, N-1\}$ is assigned an importance weight $w_i \geq 0$, and each unordered pair of distinct sentences $(i,j)$ with $0 \leq i < j < N$ is assigned a redundancy penalty $p_{ij} \geq 0$ that applies if both sentences are simultaneously selected. The objective is to maximize the total summary score, defined as the sum of importance scores of the selected sentences minus the sum of redundancy penalties incurred between all selected pairs.

Decision Variables

For each candidate sentence $i \in \{0, 1, \dots, N-1\}$, introduce a binary decision variable $x_i \in \{0, 1\}$. The variable $x_i$ equals $1$ if sentence $i$ is included in the extractive summary, and $0$ otherwise. The complete solution is represented by the vector $x = (x_0, x_1, \dots, x_{N-1}) \in \{0, 1\}^N$.
