ENERGY STORAGE SCHEDULING TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Frank Phillipson, Tom Bontekoe, and Ioan Chiscop,
"Energy Storage Scheduling: A QUBO Formulation for Quantum Computing",
Innovations for Community Services, I4CS 2021, pp. 251-261.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-030-75004-6_17

Benchmark simplification:
The benchmark uses unit charge/discharge actions, integer state of charge, and
perfect efficiency. Actions are chosen over a short horizon.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Buy low, sell high

Time steps: 3
Prices:
- t0: 1
- t1: 4
- t2: 2

Battery:
- initial state of charge: 0
- capacity: 1
- charge amount: +1
- discharge amount: -1

Allowed actions: charge, idle, discharge

Ground truth:
- Minimum net cost: -3
- Number of optimal schedules: 1
- Action sequence:
  - t0: charge (cost +1)
  - t1: discharge (revenue -4)
  - t2: idle (cost 0)
- Net cost: 1 - 4 = -3


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Two-cycle opportunity with capacity two

Time steps: 4
Prices:
- t0: 2
- t1: 5
- t2: 1
- t3: 4

Battery:
- initial state of charge: 0
- capacity: 1
- charge amount: +1
- discharge amount: -1

Allowed actions: charge, idle, discharge

Ground truth:
- Minimum net cost: -6
- Number of optimal schedules: 1
- Action sequence:
  - t0: charge (cost +2)
  - t1: discharge (revenue -5)
  - t2: charge (cost +1)
  - t3: discharge (revenue -4)
- Net cost: 2 - 5 + 1 - 4 = -6
