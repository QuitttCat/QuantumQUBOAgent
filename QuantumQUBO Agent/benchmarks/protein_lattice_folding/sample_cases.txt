HP LATTICE PROTEIN FOLDING TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Alejandro Perdomo-Ortiz, Neil Dickson, Marshall Drew-Brook, Geordie Rose, and
Alan Aspuru-Guzik,
"Finding low-energy conformations of lattice protein models by quantum
annealing",
Scientific Reports 2, Article 571, 2012.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/srep00571

Anders Irback, Lucas Knuthson, Sandipan Mohanty, and Carsten Peterson,
"Folding lattice proteins with quantum annealing",
Physical Review Research 4, 043013, 2022.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevResearch.4.043013

Benchmark simplification:
The full lattice encoding uses movement variables and self-avoidance
constraints. These cases instead provide a small candidate-conformation list and
ask for the lowest HP contact energy.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Six-residue HP sequence

Sequence: H P H H P H

Candidate conformations:
- C0:
  - nonconsecutive H-H contacts: (1,3)
  - energy: -1
- C1:
  - nonconsecutive H-H contacts: (1,4), (3,6)
  - energy: -2
- C2:
  - nonconsecutive H-H contacts: none
  - energy: 0

Ground truth:
- Minimum energy: -2
- Number of optimal conformations: 1
- Selected conformation:
  - C1


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Eight-residue HP sequence with two best folds

Sequence: H H P H P H H P

Candidate conformations:
- C0:
  - nonconsecutive H-H contacts: (1,4), (2,6)
  - energy: -2
- C1:
  - nonconsecutive H-H contacts: (1,6), (2,7), (4,7)
  - energy: -3
- C2:
  - nonconsecutive H-H contacts: (1,7), (2,4), (4,6)
  - energy: -3
- C3:
  - nonconsecutive H-H contacts: (2,6)
  - energy: -1

Ground truth:
- Minimum energy: -3
- Number of optimal conformations: 2
- Selected conformations:
  - C1
  - C2
