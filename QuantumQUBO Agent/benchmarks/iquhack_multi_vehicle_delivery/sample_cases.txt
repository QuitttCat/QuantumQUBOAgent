IQUHACK MULTI-VEHICLE DELIVERY TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
iQuHACK,
"D-Wave iQuHackathon 2025 Challenges",
GitHub repository, 2025.
URL: https://github.com/iQuHACK/2025-D-Wave

Benchmark simplification:
The iQuHACK D-Wave challenge suggests real-world quantum annealing applications
including multi-vehicle delivery, production/repair scheduling, and resource
assignment. These cases instantiate the multi-vehicle delivery idea as a
route-selection QUBO.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Two vehicles, three customer jobs

Customer jobs: J0, J1, J2
Vehicles: V0, V1

Candidate routes:
- R0: vehicle V0, covers J0 and J1, cost 6
- R1: vehicle V0, covers J2, cost 4
- R2: vehicle V1, covers J0, cost 3
- R3: vehicle V1, covers J1 and J2, cost 5

Ground truth:
- Minimum total delivery cost: 8
- Number of optimal route selections: 1
- Selected routes:
  - R2
  - R3


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Three vehicles with optional express route

Customer jobs: J0, J1, J2, J3
Vehicles: V0, V1, V2

Candidate routes:
- R0: vehicle V0, covers J0 and J1, cost 5
- R1: vehicle V0, covers J2, cost 4
- R2: vehicle V1, covers J2 and J3, cost 6
- R3: vehicle V1, covers J1, cost 3
- R4: vehicle V2, covers J3, cost 2
- R5: vehicle V2, covers J0 and J3, cost 7

Ground truth:
- Minimum total delivery cost: 11
- Number of optimal route selections: 1
- Selected routes:
  - R0
  - R2
