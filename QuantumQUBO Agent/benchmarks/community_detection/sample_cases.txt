NETWORK COMMUNITY DETECTION TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Christian F. A. Negre, Hayato Ushijima-Mwesigwa, and Susan M. Mniszewski,
"Detecting multiple communities using quantum annealing on the D-Wave system",
PLOS ONE 15(2), e0227538, 2020.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0227538

Benchmark simplification:
These cases provide candidate partitions and modularity-like scores directly.
The QUBO structure is one-hot assignment of each node to a community.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Two triangles with a weak bridge

Graph:
- Triangle A: 0-1, 1-2, 0-2
- Triangle B: 3-4, 4-5, 3-5
- Bridge: 2-3

Candidate partitions:
- P0: {0,1,2} and {3,4,5}, score 10
- P1: {0,1,2,3,4,5}, score 3
- P2: {0,1,3} and {2,4,5}, score 1

Ground truth:
- Maximum community score: 10
- Number of optimal partitions: 1
- Selected partition:
  - P0


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Three small communities

Graph:
- Community A edges: 0-1
- Community B edges: 2-3
- Community C edges: 4-5
- Cross edges: 1-2, 3-4

Candidate partitions:
- P0: {0,1}, {2,3}, {4,5}, score 8
- P1: {0,1,2,3,4,5}, score 2
- P2: {0,1,2}, {3,4,5}, score 5

Ground truth:
- Maximum community score: 8
- Number of optimal partitions: 1
- Selected partition:
  - P0
