BIN PACKING TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Lorenzo Cellini, Antonio Macaluso, and Michele Lombardi,
"QAL-BP: an augmented Lagrangian quantum approach for bin packing",
Scientific Reports 14, Article 5142, 2024.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-023-50540-3

Benchmark simplification:
These are tiny integer-weight bin packing instances kept small so that the
natural item-to-bin assignment encoding (x_{i,j}) yields at most ~12 binary
variables, allowing exact 2^n verification. The listed bins are interchangeable,
so assignments that differ only by bin labels are equivalent.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Two items, capacity five (single bin suffices)

Bin capacity: 5
Max bins available: 2
Item weights:
- I0: 2
- I1: 3

Ground truth:
- Minimum number of used bins: 1
- One optimal packing:
  - Bin B0: I0, I1 (load 5)


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Three items, capacity five (two bins needed)

Bin capacity: 5
Max bins available: 3
Item weights:
- I0: 3
- I1: 3
- I2: 2

Ground truth:
- Minimum number of used bins: 2
- One optimal packing:
  - Bin B0: I0, I2 (load 5)
  - Bin B1: I1 (load 3)
