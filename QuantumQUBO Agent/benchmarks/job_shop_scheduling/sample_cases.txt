JOB SHOP SCHEDULING TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Costantino Carugno, Maurizio Ferrari Dacrema, and Paolo Cremonesi,
"Evaluating the job shop scheduling problem on a D-wave quantum annealer",
Scientific Reports 12, Article 6539, 2022.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-022-10169-0

Riad Aggoune and Samuel Deleplanque,
"A Quantum Annealing Solution to the Job Shop Scheduling Problem",
Lecture Notes in Computer Science, ICCSA 2023 Workshops, pp. 421-428.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-031-37105-9_28

Benchmark simplification:
These are very small deterministic job-shop instances with integer durations.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Two jobs, two machines

Machines: M0, M1

Jobs:
- J0:
  - O00 on M0, duration 2
  - O01 on M1, duration 1
- J1:
  - O10 on M1, duration 2
  - O11 on M0, duration 1

Ground truth:
- Minimum makespan: 3
- One optimal schedule:
  - O00 starts at 0, runs on M0 during [0,2)
  - O10 starts at 0, runs on M1 during [0,2)
  - O01 starts at 2, runs on M1 during [2,3)
  - O11 starts at 2, runs on M0 during [2,3)


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Two jobs with a shared bottleneck machine

Machines: M0, M1

Jobs:
- J0:
  - O00 on M0, duration 1
  - O01 on M1, duration 3
- J1:
  - O10 on M1, duration 2
  - O11 on M0, duration 2

Ground truth:
- Minimum makespan: 5
- One optimal schedule:
  - O00 starts at 0, runs on M0 during [0,1)
  - O10 starts at 0, runs on M1 during [0,2)
  - O11 starts at 2, runs on M0 during [2,4)
  - O01 starts at 2, runs on M1 during [2,5)
