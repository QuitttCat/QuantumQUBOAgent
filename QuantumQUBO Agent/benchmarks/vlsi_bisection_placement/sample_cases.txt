VLSI BISECTION PLACEMENT TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Pushpalatha Pondreti and Punnam Omkaram,
"Optimization of Vlsi Physical Design Bisectional Placement with Quantum
Approximation Algorithm",
Tuijin Jishu/Journal of Propulsion Technology 46(3), pp. 1073-1084, 2025.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.52783/tjjpt.v46.i03.9844

Benchmark simplification:
These cases model VLSI bisection placement as balanced graph partitioning.
Modules are vertices and weighted nets are edges. The objective is to minimize
the cut net weight.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Four modules, two placement regions

Modules: M0, M1, M2, M3
Region sizes: 2 and 2

Weighted nets:
- M0, M1: weight 5
- M0, M2: weight 1
- M0, M3: weight 2
- M1, M2: weight 2
- M1, M3: weight 1
- M2, M3: weight 4

Ground truth:
- Minimum cut weight: 6
- Number of optimal bisections including region swaps: 2
- Optimal assignments:
  - Region A: M0, M1; Region B: M2, M3
  - Region A: M2, M3; Region B: M0, M1


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Six modules with two tight clusters

Modules: M0, M1, M2, M3, M4, M5
Region sizes: 3 and 3

Weighted nets:
- M0, M1: weight 4
- M0, M2: weight 3
- M1, M2: weight 5
- M3, M4: weight 4
- M3, M5: weight 3
- M4, M5: weight 5
- M0, M3: weight 1
- M1, M4: weight 1
- M2, M5: weight 2

Ground truth:
- Minimum cut weight: 4
- Number of optimal bisections including region swaps: 2
- Optimal assignments:
  - Region A: M0, M1, M2; Region B: M3, M4, M5
  - Region A: M3, M4, M5; Region B: M0, M1, M2
