QUANTUM CIRCUIT QUBIT ROUTING TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Friedrich Wagner, Andreas Barmann, Frauke Liers, and Markus Weissenback,
"Improving Quantum Computation by Optimized Qubit Routing",
Journal of Optimization Theory and Applications 197, pp. 1161-1194, 2023.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/s10957-023-02229-w

Takehiro Ito, Naonori Kakimura, Naoyuki Kamiyama, Yusuke Kobayashi, and Yoshio
Okamoto,
"Algorithmic Theory of Qubit Routing",
Lecture Notes in Computer Science, 2023.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-031-38906-1_35

Benchmark simplification:
These cases enumerate candidate swap sequences and their costs.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Three-qubit line

Hardware edges:
- p0-p1
- p1-p2

Initial placement:
- q0 at p0
- q1 at p2
- q2 at p1

Required gate:
- q0 with q1

Candidate routes:
- R0: no swap, cost 5
- R1: swap p1-p2, cost 1
- R2: swap p0-p1, cost 1

Ground truth:
- Minimum routing cost: 1
- Number of optimal routes: 2
- Selected routes:
  - R1
  - R2


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Four-qubit line

Hardware edges:
- p0-p1
- p1-p2
- p2-p3

Initial placement:
- q0 at p0
- q1 at p3
- q2 at p1
- q3 at p2

Required gate:
- q0 with q1

Candidate routes:
- R0: no swap, cost 9
- R1: swap p2-p3, then p1-p2, cost 2
- R2: swap p0-p1, then p1-p2, cost 2
- R3: swap p0-p1 only, cost 5

Ground truth:
- Minimum routing cost: 2
- Number of optimal routes: 2
- Selected routes:
  - R1
  - R2
