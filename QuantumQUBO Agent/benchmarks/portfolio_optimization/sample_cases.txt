PORTFOLIO OPTIMIZATION TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Harry Markowitz,
"Portfolio Selection",
The Journal of Finance 7(1), pp. 77-91, 1952.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1952.tb01525.x

Jonas Lang, Sebastian Zielinski, and Sebastian Feld,
"Strategic Portfolio Optimization Using Simulated, Digital, and Quantum
Annealing",
Applied Sciences 12(23), 12288, 2022.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.3390/app122312288

Wolfgang Sakuler et al.,
"A real-world test of portfolio optimization with quantum annealing",
Quantum Machine Intelligence, 2025.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/s42484-025-00268-2

Benchmark simplification:
These are small synthetic cardinality-constrained portfolios. Returns and
pairwise risk penalties are integer scores. The objective is return minus risk.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Four assets, choose two

Number of assets: 4
K: 2

Asset return scores:
- A0: 8
- A1: 6
- A2: 5
- A3: 4

Pairwise risk penalties:
- A0, A1: 5
- A0, A2: 1
- A0, A3: 4
- A1, A2: 2
- A1, A3: 1
- A2, A3: 1

Ground truth:
- Maximum risk-adjusted score: 12
- QUBO minimization objective value: -12
- Number of optimal solutions: 1
- Selected assets:
  - A0
  - A2
- Score calculation:
  - selected returns: 8 + 5 = 13
  - selected risk penalties: 1
  - total score: 13 - 1 = 12

TEST CASE 2: Five assets, choose three with correlated leaders

Number of assets: 5
K: 3

Asset return scores:
- A0: 7
- A1: 7
- A2: 4
- A3: 6
- A4: 3

Pairwise risk penalties:
- A0, A1: 6
- A0, A2: 1
- A0, A3: 3
- A0, A4: 1
- A1, A2: 1
- A1, A3: 2
- A1, A4: 1
- A2, A3: 1
- A2, A4: 0
- A3, A4: 1

Ground truth:
- Maximum risk-adjusted score: 13
- QUBO minimization objective value: -13
- Number of optimal solutions: 1
- Selected assets:
  - A1
  - A2
  - A3
- Score calculation:
  - selected returns: 7 + 4 + 6 = 17
  - selected risk penalties: 1 + 2 + 1 = 4
  - total score: 17 - 4 = 13
