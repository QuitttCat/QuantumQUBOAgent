VEHICLE ROUTING TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Michele Cattelan and Sheir Yarkoni,
"Modeling routing problems in QUBO with application to ride-hailing",
Scientific Reports 14, Article 19768, 2024.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-70649-3

Eneko Osaba, Esther Villar-Rodriguez, and Anton Asla,
"Solving a real-world package delivery routing problem using quantum annealers",
Scientific Reports 14, Article 24791, 2024.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-75572-1

Benchmark simplification:
Each candidate route already satisfies vehicle capacity and starts/ends at the
depot. The QUBO selects routes that cover each customer exactly once.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Two vehicles, three customers

Customers: C0, C1, C2
Vehicles: V0, V1

Candidate routes:
- R0: vehicle V0, covers C0 and C1, cost 6
- R1: vehicle V0, covers C0, cost 3
- R2: vehicle V1, covers C2, cost 4
- R3: vehicle V1, covers C1 and C2, cost 5

Ground truth:
- Minimum total route cost: 8
- Number of optimal selections: 1
- Selected routes:
  - R1
  - R3


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Four customers with bundled route

Customers: C0, C1, C2, C3
Vehicles: V0, V1

Candidate routes:
- R0: vehicle V0, covers C0 and C1, cost 5
- R1: vehicle V1, covers C2 and C3, cost 6
- R2: vehicle V1, covers C0 and C2, cost 7
- R3: vehicle V0, covers C1 and C3, cost 4
- R4: vehicle V1, covers C3, cost 3

Ground truth:
- Minimum total route cost: 11
- Number of optimal selections: 2
- Selected route sets:
  - R0, R1
  - R2, R3
