COMBINATORIAL AUCTION WINNER DETERMINATION TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Tuomas Sandholm, Subhash Suri, Andrew Gilpin, and David Levine,
"CABOB: A Fast Optimal Algorithm for Winner Determination in Combinatorial
Auctions",
Management Science 51(3), pp. 374-390, 2005.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1287/mnsc.1040.0336

Tuomas Sandholm,
"Algorithm for optimal winner determination in combinatorial auctions",
Artificial Intelligence 135(1-2), pp. 1-54, 2002.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/S0004-3702(01)00159-X

Benchmark simplification:
These cases are maximum-weight set packing instances. Each bid covers a bundle
of items and has a revenue value.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Three items, four bids

Items: A, B, C

Bids:
- B0: bundle A, revenue 4
- B1: bundle B, C, revenue 7
- B2: bundle A, B, revenue 8
- B3: bundle C, revenue 3

Ground truth:
- Maximum auction revenue: 11
- QUBO minimization objective value: -11
- Number of optimal winner sets: 1
- Selected bids:
  - B0
  - B1


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Four items with complementary bundle

Items: A, B, C, D

Bids:
- B0: bundle A, B, revenue 6
- B1: bundle C, revenue 4
- B2: bundle D, revenue 4
- B3: bundle B, C, revenue 8
- B4: bundle A, D, revenue 7

Ground truth:
- Maximum auction revenue: 11
- QUBO minimization objective value: -11
- Number of optimal winner sets: 1
- Selected bids:
  - B1
  - B4
