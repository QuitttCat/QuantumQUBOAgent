SUPPLY CHAIN ALLOCATION TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Sean J. Weinberg, Fabio Sanches, Takanori Ide, Kazumitzu Kamiya, and Randall
Correll,
"Supply chain logistics with quantum and classical annealing algorithms",
Scientific Reports 13, Article 4770, 2023.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-023-31765-8

Benchmark simplification:
These cases model capacity-limited allocation choices. Each selected assignment
uses one unit of capacity and earns a profit.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Two plants, three orders

Plant capacities:
- P0: 2
- P1: 1

Orders: O0, O1, O2

Allocation profits:
- O0 at P0: 8
- O0 at P1: 6
- O1 at P0: 5
- O1 at P1: 7
- O2 at P0: 6
- O2 at P1: 4

Constraints:
- Each order may be assigned at most once.
- Plant capacity cannot be exceeded.

Ground truth:
- Maximum profit: 21
- Number of optimal allocations: 1
- Selected allocations:
  - O0 at P0
  - O1 at P1
  - O2 at P0


========================================================================
