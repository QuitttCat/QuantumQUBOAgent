PLANAR ROBOT INVERSE KINEMATICS TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Hadi Salloum, Sergei Savin, Yaroslav Kholodov, Gleb Ryzhakov, Mirko Farina,
and Ivan Oseledets,
"Quantum annealing for inverse kinematics in robotics",
Scientific Reports 16, Article 4244, 2026.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-025-34346-z

Benchmark simplification:
The paper encodes discretized trigonometric IK constraints into QUBO. These
cases provide candidate joint configurations with precomputed error costs.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Two-link arm target

Candidate joint configurations:
- Q0: theta1=0, theta2=0, cost 5
- Q1: theta1=45, theta2=45, cost 1
- Q2: theta1=90, theta2=0, cost 3
- Q3: theta1=90, theta2=-45, cost 2

Ground truth:
- Minimum IK cost: 1
- Number of optimal configurations: 1
- Selected configuration:
  - Q1


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Tie between elbow-up and elbow-down

Candidate joint configurations:
- Q0: theta1=30, theta2=60, cost 1
- Q1: theta1=60, theta2=-60, cost 1
- Q2: theta1=0, theta2=90, cost 4
- Q3: theta1=90, theta2=0, cost 3

Ground truth:
- Minimum IK cost: 1
- Number of optimal configurations: 2
- Selected configurations:
  - Q0
  - Q1
