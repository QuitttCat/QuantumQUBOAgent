FPGA PLACEMENT TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Thore Gerlach, Stefan Knipp, David Biesner, Stelios Emmanouilidis, Klaus
Hauber, and Nico Piatkowski,
"FPGA-Placement via Quantum Annealing",
Proceedings of the 2024 ACM/SIGDA International Symposium on Field Programmable
Gate Arrays, FPGA 2024, Article 43.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/3626202.3637619


Benchmark simplification:
The paper reformulates FPGA placement as a sequence of QUBO problems solved via
adiabatic quantum computing. These cases use tiny placement grids with weighted
connections and Manhattan-distance wirelength costs.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Three blocks on three linear sites

Logic blocks: B0, B1, B2
FPGA sites:
- S0: coordinate (0, 0)
- S1: coordinate (1, 0)
- S2: coordinate (2, 0)

Weighted connections:
- B0, B1: weight 4
- B1, B2: weight 3
- B0, B2: weight 1

Ground truth:
- Minimum placement cost: 9
- Number of optimal placements: 2
- Optimal placements:
  - B0 -> S0, B1 -> S1, B2 -> S2
  - B0 -> S2, B1 -> S1, B2 -> S0
- Score calculation for first placement:
  - B0-B1: 4 * distance(S0,S1) = 4
  - B1-B2: 3 * distance(S1,S2) = 3
  - B0-B2: 1 * distance(S0,S2) = 2
  - total cost: 9


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Four blocks on a 2x2 grid

Logic blocks: B0, B1, B2, B3
FPGA sites:
- S0: coordinate (0, 0)
- S1: coordinate (1, 0)
- S2: coordinate (0, 1)
- S3: coordinate (1, 1)

Weighted connections:
- B0, B1: weight 5
- B0, B2: weight 4
- B1, B3: weight 3
- B2, B3: weight 3
- B1, B2: weight 1

Ground truth:
- Minimum placement cost: 17
- Number of optimal placements: 8
- One optimal placement:
  - B0 -> S0
  - B1 -> S1
  - B2 -> S2
  - B3 -> S3
- Score calculation:
  - B0-B1: 5 * 1 = 5
  - B0-B2: 4 * 1 = 4
  - B1-B3: 3 * 1 = 3
  - B2-B3: 3 * 1 = 3
  - B1-B2: 1 * 2 = 2
  - total cost: 17
