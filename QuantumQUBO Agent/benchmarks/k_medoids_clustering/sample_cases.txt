Christian Bauckhage, Nico Piatkowski, Rafet Sifa, Dirk Hecker, and Stefan
Wrobel,
"A QUBO Formulation of the k-Medoids Problem",
LWDA 2019, pp. 54-63.
URL: https://publica.fraunhofer.de/handle/publica/405469

Seo Woo Hong, Pierre Miasnikof, Roy Kwon, and Yuri Lawryshyn,
"Market Graph Clustering via QUBO and Digital Annealing",
Journal of Risk and Financial Management 14(1), 34, 2021.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.3390/jrfm14010034

Benchmark simplification:
These instances provide pairwise distances directly. The selected medoids are
the prototypes; each point contributes its distance to the closest selected
prototype.

K: 2

- O0, O1: 1
- O0, O2: 5
- O0, O3: 6
- O1, O2: 4
- O1, O3: 5
- O2, O3: 2

- Minimum total assignment distance: 3
- Number of optimal medoid sets: 4
- Optimal medoid sets:
  - O0, O2
  - O0, O3
  - O1, O2
  - O1, O3
- Score calculation for O0, O2:
  - O0 -> O0: 0
  - O1 -> O0: 1
  - O2 -> O2: 0
  - O3 -> O2: 2
  - total: 3
