Benchmark simplification:
The benchmark gives pairwise distances directly and uses fixed balanced cluster
sizes. The score is the sum of within-cluster pair distances.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Four points, two balanced clusters

Number of points: 4
Number of clusters: 2
Cluster sizes: 2, 2

Pairwise distances:
- P0, P1: 1
- P0, P2: 6
- P0, P3: 7
- P1, P2: 5
- P1, P3: 6
- P2, P3: 2

Ground truth:
- Minimum within-cluster distance: 3
- Number of optimal assignments including cluster-label swaps: 2
- Optimal assignments:
  - Cluster C0: P0, P1; Cluster C1: P2, P3
  - Cluster C0: P2, P3; Cluster C1: P0, P1
- Score calculation:
  - within distances: d(P0,P1) + d(P2,P3) = 1 + 2 = 3

TEST CASE 2: Six points, three balanced clusters

Number of points: 6
Number of clusters: 3
Cluster sizes: 2, 2, 2

Pairwise distances:
- P0, P1: 1
- P0, P2: 8
- P0, P3: 7
- P0, P4: 9
- P0, P5: 8
- P1, P2: 7
- P1, P3: 6
- P1, P4: 8
- P1, P5: 9
- P2, P3: 2
- P2, P4: 7
- P2, P5: 8
- P3, P4: 6
- P3, P5: 7
- P4, P5: 1

Ground truth:
- Minimum within-cluster distance: 4
- Number of optimal assignments including cluster-label permutations: 6
- Unlabeled optimal clusters:
  - P0, P1
  - P2, P3
  - P4, P5
- Score calculation:
  - within distances: 1 + 2 + 1 = 4
