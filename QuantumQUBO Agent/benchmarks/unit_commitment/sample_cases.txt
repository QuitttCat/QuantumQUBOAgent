UNIT COMMITMENT TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Zhenyu Li et al.,
"Hybrid quantum annealing decomposition framework for unit commitment",
Electric Power Systems Research 238, 111121, 2025.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.epsr.2024.111121

Benchmark simplification:
The full unit commitment problem includes ramping, minimum up/down times, and
network constraints. These cases focus on binary on/off decisions, capacity,
and cost.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: One period, two generators

Demand: 5

Generators:
- G0: capacity 5, fixed on-cost 3, variable cost 1 per unit
- G1: capacity 5, fixed on-cost 1, variable cost 2 per unit

Ground truth:
- Minimum total cost: 8
- Unit schedule and dispatch:
  - G0 on, produces 5
  - G1 off, produces 0
- Score calculation:
  - fixed cost 3 + production cost 5 = 8


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Two periods with changing demand

Demands:
- t0: 3
- t1: 6

Generators:
- G0: capacity 4, fixed on-cost 2 per period, variable cost 1 per unit
- G1: capacity 4, fixed on-cost 1 per period, variable cost 3 per unit

Ground truth:
- Minimum total cost: 18
- Unit schedule and dispatch:
  - t0: G0 on produces 3, G1 off produces 0
  - t1: G0 on produces 4, G1 on produces 2
- Score calculation:
  - t0 cost: fixed 2 + production 3 = 5
  - t1 cost: fixed 2 + fixed 1 + production 4 + production 6 = 13
  - total cost: 18
