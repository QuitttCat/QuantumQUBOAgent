Cryptanalysis Prime Factorization Toy Problem

Given a small composite integer N and candidate factor pairs, choose the factor
pair whose product equals N. Candidate pairs can be scored by squared product
error, with optional penalties for trivial factors.

Task:
Choose exactly one candidate factor pair.

Objective:
Minimize product error:

  score = (p * q - N)^2 + optional penalties.

Goal:
Return the selected factor pair and minimum score. A score of 0 means the
selected pair satisfies all included constraints.

QUBO convention:
Full factorization QUBOs encode multiplication constraints over binary factor
bits. These toy cases provide candidate factor pairs with precomputed errors.
