CRYPTANALYSIS PRIME FACTORIZATION TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Shuxian Jiang, Keith A. Britt, Alexander J. McCaskey, Travis S. Humble, and
Sabre Kais,
"Quantum Annealing for Prime Factorization",
Scientific Reports 8, Article 17667, 2018.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-018-36058-z

Raouf Dridi and Hedayat Alghassi,
"Prime factorization using quantum annealing and computational algebraic
geometry",
Scientific Reports 7, Article 43048, 2017.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/srep43048

Benchmark simplification:
The full QUBO encodes binary multiplication constraints. These cases are small
candidate-selection versions for testing whether solvers can choose the
zero-error factor pair.

Target composite N: 15

Candidate factor pairs:
- C0: p=1, q=15, product error 0
- C1: p=3, q=5, product error 0
- C2: p=2, q=7, product error 1
- C3: p=4, q=4, product error 1

Ground truth:
- Minimum product error: 0
- Number of optimal factor pairs: 2
- Selected candidate pairs:
  - C0
