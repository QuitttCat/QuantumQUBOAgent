Benchmark simplification:
The Mars observation scheduling paper formulates a QUBO for scheduling MARSIS
radar observations onboard Mars Express, using small instances derived from real
operations data. These benchmark cases abstract the mission into candidate
observation windows with science scores and explicit conflict pairs.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: South-pole radar passes with one overlap conflict

Candidate observations:
- O0:
  - target: SouthPole_A
  - time window: orbit 101, slot 0
  - science score: 8
- O1:
  - target: SouthPole_B
  - time window: orbit 101, slot 1
  - science score: 6
- O2:
  - target: SouthPole_C
  - time window: orbit 102, slot 0
  - science score: 5
- O3:
  - target: SouthPole_D
  - time window: orbit 102, slot 1
  - science score: 4

Conflicting observation pairs:
- O0 conflicts with O1
- O2 conflicts with O3

Ground truth:
- Maximum science score: 13
- QUBO minimization objective value: -13
- Number of optimal schedules: 1
- Selected observations:
  - O0
  - O2
- Score calculation:
  - 8 + 5 = 13
