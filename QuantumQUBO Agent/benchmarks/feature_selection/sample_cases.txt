FEATURE SELECTION TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Sascha Mucke, Raoul Heese, Sabine Muller, Moritz Wolter, and Nico Piatkowski,
"Feature selection on quantum computers",
Quantum Machine Intelligence 5, Article 11, 2023.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/s42484-023-00099-z

Daniel Pranjic, Bharadwaj Chowdary Mummaneni, and Christian Tutschku,
"Quantum annealing-based feature selection",
Neurocomputing, 2025.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neucom.2025.131673

Benchmark simplification:
These are small synthetic relevance-redundancy instances. Relevance values can
be interpreted as mutual information with the label. Redundancy values penalize
selecting two similar features together.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Four features, two equally good nonredundant choices

Number of features: 4
K: 2

Feature relevance scores:
- F0: 9
- F1: 7
- F2: 6
- F3: 5

Pairwise redundancy penalties:
- F0, F1: 6
- F0, F2: 2
- F0, F3: 1
- F1, F2: 1
- F1, F3: 3
- F2, F3: 1

Ground truth:
- Maximum feature-selection score: 13
- QUBO minimization objective value: -13
- Number of optimal solutions: 2
- Selected feature sets:
  - F0, F2
  - F0, F3
- Score calculation:
  - F0, F2: 9 + 6 - 2 = 13
  - F0, F3: 9 + 5 - 1 = 13

Number of features: 5
K: 3

Feature relevance scores:
- F0: 5

- F2: 4

- F4: 6

Pairwise redundancy penalties:
- F0, F1: 2
- F0, F2: 1

- F1, F2: 1
- F1, F3: 5

Ground truth:
- Maximum feature-selection score: 15
- QUBO minimization objective value: -15
- Number of optimal solutions: 1
- Selected features:
  - F1
  - F2
  - F4
- Score calculation:
  - selected relevance: 8 + 4 + 6 = 18
  - selected redundancy: 1 + 2 + 0 = 3
  - total score: 18 - 3 = 15
