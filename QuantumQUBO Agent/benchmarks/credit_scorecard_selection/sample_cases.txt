CREDIT SCORECARD COMBINATION TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Fengyuan Ma, Yujie He, and Juan Hu,
"Application of QUBO model in credit score card combination optimization",
Highlights in Science, Engineering and Technology 68, 2023.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.54097/hset.v68i.12092

Benchmark simplification:
These cases model scorecard combination as fixed-cardinality binary selection
with pairwise overlap penalties.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Three scorecards from four

Number of scorecards: 4
K: 2

Revenue scores:
- S0_low_risk: 8
- S1_balanced: 7
- S2_growth: 6
- S3_high_risk: 9

Overlap penalties:
- S0_low_risk, S1_balanced: 3
- S0_low_risk, S2_growth: 1
- S0_low_risk, S3_high_risk: 4
- S1_balanced, S2_growth: 1
- S1_balanced, S3_high_risk: 2
- S2_growth, S3_high_risk: 3

Ground truth:
- Maximum revenue score: 14
- QUBO minimization objective value: -14
- Number of optimal solutions: 1
- Selected scorecards:
  - S1_balanced
  - S3_high_risk
