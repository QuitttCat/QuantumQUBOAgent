DATABASE SERIALIZABILITY VERIFICATION TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Natsuki Hamada, Kazuhiro Saito, and Hideyuki Kawashima,
"Qobra: Fast Verification of Transactional Serializability with Quantum
Annealing",
Proceedings of the 2023 IEEE International Conference on Big Data, BigData
2023, pp. 1322-1329.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1109/BigData59044.2023.10386188

Natsuki Hamada, Kazuhiro Saito, and Hideyuki Kawashima,
"Qobra: Accelerating Transactional Serializability Verifier by Quantum
Annealing",
Journal of Information Processing 32, pp. 1013-1022, 2024.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.2197/ipsjjip.32.1013

Benchmark simplification:
The Qobra papers formulate black-box serializability verification as a
combinatorial optimization problem suitable for quantum annealing. These cases
provide small transaction-order candidates and dependency-violation counts.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Serializable three-transaction execution

Transactions:
- T0
- T1
- T2

Dependency constraints:
- T0 must precede T1
- T1 must precede T2

Candidate serial orders:
- O0: T0, T1, T2
  - violation count: 0
- O1: T0, T2, T1
  - violation count: 1
- O2: T1, T0, T2
  - violation count: 1
- O3: T2, T1, T0
  - violation count: 2

Ground truth:
- Minimum dependency violations: 0
- Number of optimal orders: 1
- Selected order:
  - O0
