HOSPITAL STAFF RESCHEDULING TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
"Real-Time Healthcare Workforce Rescheduling using a Quantum Computer: A Novel
Approach to Dynamic Staff Allocation in Hospital Settings",
European Journal of Computer Science and Information Technology 13(29), 2025.
URL: https://eajournals.org/ejcsit/vol13-issue29-2025/real-time-healthcare-workforce-rescheduling-using-a-quantum-computer-a-novel-approach-to-dynamic-staff-allocation-in-hospital-settings/

Kazuki Ikeda, Yuma Nakamura, and Travis S. Humble,
"Application of Quantum Annealing to Nurse Scheduling Problem",
Scientific Reports 9, Article 12837, 2019.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-019-49172-3

Benchmark simplification:
These cases model emergency rescheduling after absences. Qualification filtering
is already applied in the feasible assignment table. Costs combine overtime,
fatigue, travel, and preference penalties into one integer score.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: One ICU absence

Uncovered shifts:
- ICU_Night

Available qualified staff and costs:
- Nurse_A -> ICU_Night: cost 4
- Nurse_B -> ICU_Night: cost 2
- Nurse_C -> ICU_Night: cost 5

Ground truth:
- Minimum total rescheduling cost: 2
- Number of optimal schedules: 1
- Assignments:
  - ICU_Night -> Nurse_B


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Two simultaneous absences with shared candidate

Uncovered shifts:
- ICU_Night
- ER_Evening

Available qualified assignments and costs:
- Nurse_A -> ICU_Night: cost 3
- Nurse_A -> ER_Evening: cost 4
- Nurse_B -> ICU_Night: cost 2
- Nurse_C -> ER_Evening: cost 1
- Nurse_D -> ER_Evening: cost 3

Constraints:
- Each uncovered shift must receive exactly one nurse.
- A nurse may cover at most one uncovered shift.

Ground truth:
- Minimum total rescheduling cost: 3
- Number of optimal schedules: 1
- Assignments:
  - ICU_Night -> Nurse_B
  - ER_Evening -> Nurse_C
- Score calculation:
  - 2 + 1 = 3
