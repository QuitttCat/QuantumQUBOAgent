CLINICAL ENZYME TARGET IDENTIFICATION TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Hoang M. Ngo, My T. Thai, and Tamer Kahveci,
"QuTIE: quantum optimization for target identification by enzymes",
Bioinformatics Advances 3(1), vbad112, 2023.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1093/bioadv/vbad112

Benchmark simplification:
The QuTIE paper formulates target identification by enzymes as a QUBO problem
and evaluates disease-related metabolic networks from KEGG, including human
disease classes. These cases reduce the setting to cardinality-constrained
enzyme target selection with pathway-overlap penalties.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Two enzyme targets for a disease pathway

Candidate enzymes:
- E0:
  - pathway role: upstream substrate supply
  - benefit score: 8
- E1:
  - pathway role: branch reaction
  - benefit score: 6
- E2:
  - pathway role: downstream disease product
  - benefit score: 7
- E3:
  - pathway role: compensatory side route
  - benefit score: 5

Task:
- Choose exactly K = 2 enzyme targets.

Pairwise redundancy penalties:
- E0, E1: 1
- E0, E2: 3
- E0, E3: 1
- E1, E2: 1
- E1, E3: 2
- E2, E3: 1

Ground truth:
- Maximum target score: 13
- QUBO minimization objective value: -13
- Number of optimal target sets: 1
- Selected targets:
  - E0
  - E1
- Score calculation:
  - E0, E1: 8 + 6 - 1 = 13


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Three targets with overlapping pathway coverage

Candidate enzymes:
- E0:
  - pathway role: primary disease flux
  - benefit score: 9
- E1:
  - pathway role: alternate disease flux
  - benefit score: 7
- E2:
  - pathway role: cofactor regeneration
  - benefit score: 5
- E3:
  - pathway role: terminal product formation
  - benefit score: 8
- E4:
  - pathway role: weak peripheral reaction
  - benefit score: 4

Task:
- Choose exactly K = 3 enzyme targets.

Pairwise redundancy penalties:
- E0, E1: 4
- E0, E2: 1
- E0, E3: 2
- E0, E4: 1
- E1, E2: 1
- E1, E3: 1
- E1, E4: 1
- E2, E3: 1
- E2, E4: 0
- E3, E4: 2

Ground truth:
- Maximum target score: 18
- QUBO minimization objective value: -18
- Number of optimal target sets: 1
- Selected targets:
  - E0
  - E2
  - E3
- Score calculation:
  - benefit: 9 + 5 + 8 = 22
  - redundancy: 1 + 2 + 1 = 4
  - total target score: 18
