ASSET EXCHANGE QUBO-PLUS TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Fred Glover, Gary Kochenberger, Moses Ma, and Yu Du,
"Quantum Bridge Analytics II: QUBO-Plus, network optimization and combinatorial
chaining for asset exchange",
Annals of Operations Research 314, pp. 185-212, 2022.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/s10479-022-04695-3

Fred Glover, Gary Kochenberger, Rick Hennig, and Yu Du,
"Quantum bridge analytics I: a tutorial on formulating and using QUBO models",
Annals of Operations Research 314, pp. 141-183, 2022.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/s10479-022-04634-2

Benchmark simplification:
The full Quantum Bridge Analytics II paper introduces QUBO-Plus and studies
asset exchange using network optimization and combinatorial chaining. These
benchmark cases reduce the asset exchange setting to selecting compatible
candidate exchanges with maximum surplus.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Two disjoint bilateral exchanges

Participants: P0, P1, P2, P3

Candidate exchanges:
- X0:
  - participants: P0, P1
  - assets exchanged: A0, A1
  - surplus score: 8
- X1:
  - participants: P1, P2
  - assets exchanged: A1, A2
  - surplus score: 6
- X2:
  - participants: P2, P3
  - assets exchanged: A2, A3
  - surplus score: 5
- X3:
  - participants: P0, P3
  - assets exchanged: A0, A3
  - surplus score: 4

Conflicts:
- Exchanges sharing a participant conflict.
- Exchanges sharing an asset conflict.

Ground truth:
- Maximum total surplus: 13
- QUBO minimization objective value: -13
- Number of optimal selections: 1
- Selected exchanges:
  - X0
  - X2
- Score calculation:
  - surplus: 8 + 5 = 13
