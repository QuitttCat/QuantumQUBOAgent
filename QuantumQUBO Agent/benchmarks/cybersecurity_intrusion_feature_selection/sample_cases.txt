NETWORK INTRUSION FEATURE SELECTION TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
I. Pathak, T. Kim, N. Yilmazer, and D. Roach,
"Feature Selection for Imbalanced Network Intrusion Detection Systems Dataset
Using Quantum Annealer",
IEEE International Conference on Electro Information Technology, pp. 161-166,
2025.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1109/EIT64391.2025.11103662

Jianhua Yang, Yanchun Liang, and Xiangyang Wang,
"Quantum Particle swarm optimization based network Intrusion feature selection
and Detection",
IFAC Proceedings Volumes 41(2), pp. 12312-12317, 2008.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.3182/20080706-5-KR-1001.02084

Benchmark simplification:
These cases use relevance-redundancy scores for intrusion-detection features.
They mirror the QUBO feature-selection structure used for high-dimensional
network traffic data.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Four traffic features, choose two

Number of features: 4
K: 2

Detection relevance scores:
- F0_packet_rate: 8
- F1_failed_logins: 7
- F2_byte_ratio: 5
- F3_port_entropy: 6

Pairwise redundancy penalties:
- F0_packet_rate, F1_failed_logins: 1
- F0_packet_rate, F2_byte_ratio: 4
- F0_packet_rate, F3_port_entropy: 2
- F1_failed_logins, F2_byte_ratio: 1
- F1_failed_logins, F3_port_entropy: 3
- F2_byte_ratio, F3_port_entropy: 1

Ground truth:
- Maximum feature score: 14
- QUBO minimization objective value: -14
- Number of optimal solutions: 1
- Selected features:
  - F0_packet_rate
  - F1_failed_logins
- Score calculation:
  - relevance: 8 + 7 = 15
  - redundancy: 1
  - total score: 14

- F0_duration, F2_dst_bytes: 1

- F0_duration, F4_service_entropy: 2
- F1_src_bytes, F2_dst_bytes: 6
- F1_src_bytes, F3_login_failures: 1
- F1_src_bytes, F4_service_entropy: 2
- F2_dst_bytes, F3_login_failures: 1

- F3_login_failures, F4_service_entropy: 1
