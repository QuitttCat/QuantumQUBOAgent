NURSE SCHEDULING TEST CASES

Source inspiration:
Kazuki Ikeda, Yuma Nakamura, and Travis S. Humble,
"Application of Quantum Annealing to Nurse Scheduling Problem",
Scientific Reports 9, Article 12837, 2019.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-019-49172-3

Benchmark simplification:
The full nurse scheduling problem can include fairness, rest periods, forbidden
patterns, and requested days off. These cases keep the QUBO core: one nurse per
shift, limited nurse capacity, and assignment costs.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Three nurses, three shifts

Nurses: N0, N1, N2
Shifts: S0, S1, S2

Assignment costs:
- N0: S0=1, S1=4, S2=3
- N1: S0=2, S1=1, S2=5
- N2: S0=3, S1=2, S2=1

Ground truth:
- Minimum total cost: 3
- Number of optimal schedules: 1
- Assignments:
  - S0 -> N0
  - S1 -> N1
  - S2 -> N2
- Score calculation:
  - 1 + 1 + 1 = 3


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Four nurses, three shifts with one spare nurse

Nurses: N0, N1, N2, N3
Shifts: S0, S1, S2

Assignment costs:
- N0: S0=4, S1=1, S2=5
- N1: S0=2, S1=3, S2=2
- N2: S0=1, S1=4, S2=3
- N3: S0=3, S1=2, S2=1

Ground truth:
- Minimum total cost: 3
- Number of optimal schedules: 1
- Assignments:
  - S0 -> N2
  - S1 -> N0
  - S2 -> N3
- Score calculation:
  - 1 + 1 + 1 = 3
