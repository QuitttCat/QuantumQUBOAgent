Benchmark simplification:
Each train has a small set of discrete timing options. Explicit conflict pairs
represent single-track incompatibilities.


========================================================================
TEST CASE 1: Two trains meeting on a single track

Trains: T0, T1

Timing options:
- T0_Early: delay cost 0
- T0_Late: delay cost 2
- T1_Early: delay cost 0
- T1_Late: delay cost 2

Conflicting option pairs:
- T0_Early conflicts with T1_Early

Ground truth:
- Minimum feasible delay cost: 2
- Number of optimal dispatches: 2
- Optimal dispatches:
  - T0_Early, T1_Late
  - T0_Late, T1_Early


========================================================================
TEST CASE 2: Three trains, cascading conflict

Trains: T0, T1, T2

Timing options:
- T0_0: delay cost 0
- T0_1: delay cost 1
- T1_0: delay cost 0
- T1_1: delay cost 2
- T2_0: delay cost 0
- T2_1: delay cost 1

Conflicting option pairs:
- T0_0 conflicts with T1_0
- T1_1 conflicts with T2_0
- T0_1 conflicts with T2_1

Ground truth:
- Minimum feasible delay cost: 1
- Number of optimal dispatches: 1
- Selected options:
  - T0_1
  - T1_0
  - T2_0
