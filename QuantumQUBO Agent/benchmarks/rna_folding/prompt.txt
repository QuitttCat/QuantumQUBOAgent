RNA Secondary-Structure Stem Selection Problem

RNA molecules fold by forming hydrogen-bonded base pairs between nucleotides.
Common canonical or near-canonical pairs are:
- A-U
- G-C
- G-U, also called a wobble pair

Consecutive base pairs can form a stem, also called a helix. In this benchmark,
the input already lists a small set of candidate stems. Each candidate stem has:
- a stem ID,
- a list of nucleotide base pairs,
- a positive stability score.

Higher stability scores are better. The task is to choose a subset of candidate
stems that gives the largest total stability score while respecting RNA
secondary-structure compatibility constraints.

Two stems are incompatible if they:
- reuse at least one nucleotide position, or
- are explicitly listed as conflicting in the input.

For this benchmark, use the supplied conflict list as authoritative. If two
stems appear in the conflict list, they must not both be selected. If two stems
do not appear in the conflict list, they may be selected together.

Input:
- An RNA sequence with 1-indexed nucleotide positions.
- A list of candidate stems.
- A list of pairwise conflicts between candidate stems.

Task:
Choose a subset of candidate stems.

Constraints:
- A stem can either be selected or not selected.
- No pair of conflicting stems may both be selected.

Objective:
Maximize the total stability score of the selected stems:

total score =
  sum of stability scores for all selected stems.

Goal:
Return the selected stems and the maximum total stability score.

QUBO convention:
The natural problem is a maximization problem. If using a minimization QUBO,
the unconstrained objective should minimize the negative selected stability
score, with sufficiently large quadratic penalties for conflicting stem pairs.
