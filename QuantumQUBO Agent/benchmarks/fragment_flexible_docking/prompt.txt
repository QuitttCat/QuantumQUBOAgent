Fragment-Based Flexible Docking Placement Selection Problem

In fragment-based protein-ligand flexible docking, a ligand is decomposed into
rigid fragments. For each fragment, several candidate placements inside the
protein binding pocket are generated. The task is to choose one placement for
each fragment so that the selected placements form a physically plausible ligand
pose.

Each candidate placement has a protein-fragment interaction energy. Lower,
more negative energies are better.

Pairs of selected placements also interact:
- If two placements geometrically clash, a nonnegative clash penalty is added.
- If the corresponding fragments are chemically bonded and the two placements
  are geometrically compatible, a nonnegative bond reward is subtracted.

Input:
- A list of ligand fragments.
- For each fragment:
  - a list of candidate placements,
  - the interaction energy of each candidate placement.
- A list of pairwise placement terms between candidates belonging to different
  fragments. Each pairwise term gives:
  - the first placement,
  - the second placement,
  - a clash penalty,
  - a bond reward.

Task:
For each fragment, choose one and only one candidate placement from that
fragment's own placement list. Do not choose placements from another fragment's
list, and do not choose more than one placement for the same fragment.

Objective:
Minimize the total docking score:

total score =
  sum of selected placement interaction energies
  + sum of clash penalties between selected placement pairs
  - sum of bond rewards between selected placement pairs.

Goal:
Return the selected placement for each fragment and the minimum total docking
score.
